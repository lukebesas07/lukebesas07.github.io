What is High Stakes?

The VEX V5 Robotics Competition: High Stakes is played between two alliances, red and blue, each consisting of two teams, on a 12’ x 12’ field featuring a central ladder, stakes, mobile goals, and rings. The objective is to score more points than the opposing alliance by placing rings on stakes, positioning mobile goals, and climbing the ladder. Each ring scored on a stake is worth one point, while the top ring on a stake is worth three points. The mobile goals are hexagonal objects with a maximum diameter of 10 inches and an overall height of 14.5 inches. At the center of the field, the ladder has three levels and includes one high stake for additional challenge. Matches are divided into a 15-second autonomous period followed by a 1 minute and 45-second driver-controlled period. According to the rules, rings may only be counted once for scoring, and during the final 15 seconds of the match, teams are prohibited from touching scored rings or mobile goals in the positive corners.
